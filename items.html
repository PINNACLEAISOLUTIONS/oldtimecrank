Victor 5 Phonograph

$2,000.00

The Victor 5 is well known for its handsome quarter sawn oak cabinet and this example retains its original finish. All original nickel plating is in excellent shape. The triple spring motor runs strong and smooth; it will play several records with ease.

The backmount has its original striping and is in near mint condition. This machine comes with a stunning brass bell horn measuring 19 1/2" across the bell and 29 1/2" long. The horn has been professionally straightened and polished; it presents beautifully.

The original Victor decal is in nice condition. The only replaced item is the turntable felt. The Exhibition reproducer has been rebuilt and plays excellently.

These machines were produced between 1906 and 1920. This Victor 5 stands as a wonderful example of Victor craftsmanship from that era.

  • Original quarter sawn oak cabinet with original finish
  • Triple spring motor, runs strong and smooth
  • Brass bell horn (19 1/2" x 29 1/2"), professionally polished
  • Exhibition reproducer, rebuilt and plays excellently
  • Includes several acoustic records
  • Includes new old stock needle tin with 200 needles

Herzog 1/2 Round Oak Cylinder Cabinet

$1,750.00

Up for sale is a beautiful Herzog 1/2 Round Oak Cylinder Cabinet. These cabinets were made around 1905 to 1910 by the Herzog Furniture Company based in Saginaw, Michigan.

This 1/2 round holds approximately 120 two minute or four minute cylinders on cardboard tubes. The cabinet has been refinished many years ago and has stunning quarter sawn oak veneers. The finish is in very nice condition. The interior still retains the original cardboard pegs.

If you have an Edison Triumph or Opera or any oak phonograph that you would like to put on this cabinet, it will look magnificent.

  • Holds approximately 120 cylinders
  • Stunning quarter sawn oak veneers
  • Original cardboard pegs intact
  • Dimensions: 33" tall x 19" wide x 19" deep
  • Free local pickup available

Salter Quarter Round Record Cabinet

$1,900.00

This is a great cabinet made by the Salter Furniture Company. There were many record cabinets made around the early 1900's but very few of this style.

It is called a Quarter Round because of its very narrow construction, measuring only 16 1/2" wide and 18" deep. Also a little taller than most at 37 3/4" high. The top measures 17 1/4" x 20" deep.

The cabinet has a new interior that has been done with 14 new vertical slots to hold either 10 inch or 12 inch records. The cabinet has been professionally restored and has stunning red and black mahogany striped veneer on the curved front door.

Cabinet also has beautiful corner columns on each side with hand carving above each column. The Salter Record Cabinet would make a great combination with a Victor 6 or a Victor 4 sitting on top of it.

  • Rare quarter round style cabinet
  • Dimensions: 37 3/4" tall x 16 1/2" wide x 18" deep
  • Top measures 17 1/4" x 20" deep
  • 14 vertical slots for 10" or 12" records
  • Professionally restored
  • Stunning red and black mahogany striped veneer
  • Hand-carved corner columns

Shipping: $300 in the USA only.
